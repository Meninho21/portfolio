After completing High school I started my Bachelor in IT at the university of Luxembourg. The first year the programming language course, was Java through Visual Studio Code. Consequently Git and Github was also introduced. We had one course that introduced us to Javafx and later Android Studio. The idea of running my own Software on my phone quickly sparked my interest. Later, we started working with Javascript (Typescript), C and Swift. Around 2 year of university, the advent of ChatGPT quickly impacted our entire class. Students that wanted an easy way to pass the course jumped on the opportunity to use it to cheat. Still at its early stages, it became quickly apparent, that it wasn't quite mature yet for coding and so it didn't really work that well. In the cases where AI did work well enough, the university was quick to implement AI detectors to catch the cheaters. First very sceptical of AI and being on the fence about using it for myself, since I couldn't really see it being very useful except for having it do your work and sometimes creating entirely non-working results. It got clear to me that things weren't that simple. I started relying on AI as I kind of a faster way to research information. AI adapts its answers to the user using it, which is much more effective when doing research in specific cases. The more specific your questions are, the more effectively the AI can answer in contrast to a Google search in a wide variety of cases. The time gain from using AI in your work is no joke, but only if it's done correctly. If you aren't learning while you are coding using AI you are very likely using it wrong. AI is best used when it helps you find mistakes that would otherwise take you a long time to find. To suggest ideas of how your work could be done more efficiently.
